This is a small experiment written in the weekend after I was given my main task at my internship, which ultimately led to the "Isochrone" tool in JPL's MMGIS app. I figured an introductory exercise could also be an opportunity to experiment with a technique I'd been interested in for a while: terrain generation with Perlin noise. Red areas are high elevation, blue areas are low elevation. The purple shaded area roughly represents the area reachable from the center within some arbitrary constraint, based on an arbitrary cost function.

It's not terribly fast, useful, or feature-rich. But I think it's neat.
